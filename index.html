With so many diets out there that it is hard to keep track of them all — from Whole 30, to the Paleo Diet, to the DASH Diet… the list goes on and on. Diets are talked about on various platforms including newspapers, personal blogs, social media and it seems like new ones emerge every month.

While some diets are prescribed for medical reasons, such as controling diabetes or blood pressure, there are also many diets that focus primarily on weight loss.

Let’s break-down how Americans search for diets and dieting.



Search interest for dieting has decreased since 2004. This could be due to the health trends focused on living a healthy life.

According to the CDC: “The key to achieving and maintaining a healthy weight isn't about short‐term dietary changes. It's about a lifestyle that includes healthy eating, regular physical activity, and balancing the number of calories you consume with the number of calories your body uses.”

While dieting has become less popular over time, there are still some trends that exist within the data.

As you can see below, there is a pattern when people in the United States search for diets. This search interest peaks each year in January, most likely due to new years resolutions.There is also a small peak that occurs in the summer months.
